FAQ

What is Breaking Bad about?
Breaking Bad is a TV series that follows Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher turned methamphetamine manufacturer, after he's diagnosed with terminal cancer. He partners with former student Jesse Pinkman to produce and sell meth, leading to a dark and violent descent into the criminal underworld.
Who created Breaking Bad?
The series was created by Vince Gilligan. It originally aired on AMC from 2008 to 2013, spanning five seasons.
Why does Walter White start making meth?
Walter starts making meth to secure his family’s financial future after being diagnosed with terminal lung cancer. Initially, his intentions are good, but over time, his motivations become increasingly driven by pride, power, and ego.
What is the significance of the blue meth?
The blue meth is significant because it represents the high-quality product that Walter, known as “Heisenberg,” produces. Its purity sets it apart in the drug market and becomes a symbol of Walter’s exceptional chemistry skills and his descent into the drug world.
